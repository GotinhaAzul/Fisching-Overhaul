# Fisching Overhaul Progression Guide: First Run to 100%

This guide is meant to feel like a real playthrough. It is not a speedrun route, not a low-spoiler mystery route, and not a spreadsheet. The goal is to help a player move from the opening pools to the endgame while quietly setting up a full-completion file instead of reaching the final stretch with half the game still unfinished.

It is written to stay spoiler-light on hidden content and late secrets, but practical about what actually blocks progress. When a mission wall has a specific requirement, this guide names it and tells you where to find it. That information is only given at the moment it becomes relevant — not front-loaded.

## How to use this guide

Each stage in this guide is a self-contained chapter. You can open any stage mid-run and get everything you need for that phase without flipping to the back. The reference sections at the end cover money-farming, crafting, mutations, and hunts as complete lookups — but every stage also tells you what matters for that specific moment in the run.

- Treat each stage like a phase of the run, not a rigid checklist.
- Push forward when your current pool is still giving XP, money, and bestiary progress at the same time.
- Rotate away when a branch starts asking for one very specific thing and everything else in that pool has slowed down.
- Finish pools that are already close to 100% instead of carrying lots of nearly-finished work into the postgame.
- Keep at least one copy of unusual fish whenever possible until you are sure they are not tied to missions, hunts, crafting, or appraise targets.
- When you see a "Key bottlenecks" block in a stage, that is where the guide gets specific. Read it when you hit a wall, not necessarily before.

## What "100%" means here

For the core route, this guide assumes you want:

- all 25 main bestiary pools at 100%
- all 34 missions completed
- all hunts cleared at least once
- all 17 crafting recipes completed
- all rods that count toward rod completion collected
- the global fish, rod, and pool bestiary rewards claimed

The repo has 27 pools total, but 2 hidden pools are excluded from main bestiary completion. Some tribute or secret rods are also excluded from rod bestiary completion. Because of that, this guide treats hidden late-game content as bonus completion after the core route is already in good shape. A dedicated section at the end covers the tribute rods for players who want everything.

## The four-part rhythm of a healthy run

Most files feel best when you rotate between four kinds of progress:

1. Mission progress that unlocks a new pool, rod, or branch.
2. Bestiary progress in pools that are currently relevant.
3. Side power from rods, crafting, upgrades, appraise, or mutation projects.
4. Cleanup in older zones that are already almost solved.

If you keep those four moving together, the game feels broad and rewarding. If you ignore one of them for too long, the run usually becomes either grindy or messy. The most common mistake is treating categories 1 and 2 as the only real progress and leaving 3 and 4 for the postgame. By the time you reach the endgame, that backlog can feel overwhelming.

Think of the four rhythms as a pulse. Each session, at least two of them should move. Three is better. All four at once means you are playing very efficiently.

## Before the route: what actually drives progression

Progression is not only about levels.

- Levels matter because they gate several major missions, including some that cannot be rushed regardless of how much money you have.
- Bestiary percent matters because many pools open from discovery, not money alone.
- Mutations matter because some branches ask for them directly, and the rarest ones require intentional setup, not just luck.
- Rod comfort matters because the minigame becomes a real skill check in midgame and beyond. A rod you trust beats a rod with better stats that you fumble.
- Crafting matters because some rods are not just upgrades — they are solutions to later problems, and some of the best ones cannot be bought at all. Several crafts also have ingredients scattered across multiple zones, so the sooner you start banking the pieces, the smoother the build feels when it is actually ready.
- Money matters more than it looks early on. Several pool unlocks have money costs that are easy to overlook, and a few endgame gates are expensive.

That is why this guide often tells you to revisit a pool instead of hard-rushing the newest unlock.

## General principles that save time

### Value control early

In the early and midgame, `control` often matters more than headline luck. A rod that feels stable will produce better XP, cleaner catches, and less frustration than a stronger-looking rod that makes every tough fish stressful. Upgrading control on a rod you already trust is often a better investment than chasing a new one.

### Do not appraise random filler

Appraise matters most when the fish already has a reason to matter:

- it is part of a mission wall
- it is a useful mutation target
- it is tied to a craft
- it is a valuable rare catch worth refining

Appraise is not a shortcut through rod-exclusive mutation lines, and it is not an efficient use of money on common fish you will catch dozens of anyway.

### Upgrade rods you actually use

Rod upgrades cost real money and fish materials. Use them on rods you expect to keep active for a while.

- upgrade `control` when catches are getting tense
- upgrade `luck` when a rod is carrying a wide slice of progression
- upgrade `kg_max` when you are losing fish that would otherwise matter

### Do not sell everything

A run gets much smoother if you stop liquidating every catch the moment you get it. Missions, crafting, hunts, and upgrades all reward the player who keeps a little inventory depth. Get into the habit of holding one extra copy of anything rare or unusual. The cost of keeping it is low. The cost of needing it later and having nothing is a whole farming session.

### Watch your money alongside your level

Several pool unlocks have money costs that are easy to underestimate. Grande Recife, Geleira Snowcap, Baia do Sol, and the Marianas Prologue all require spending real money in addition to meeting level or bestiary thresholds. Running out of cash right before an unlock you are otherwise ready for is a common friction point that a little awareness prevents.

### Bestiary cleanup is strongest when done in waves

A pool at 92% is worth much more attention than a pool at 18%. The fastest way to reach full completion is usually to finish clusters of nearly-complete pools along the way, not to postpone every cleanup task for the end.

### Know which mutations are rod-exclusive

Several of the most important mutations in the game will never appear unless you have the right rod equipped. `Brilhante` requires `Vara Brilhante`. `Envenenado` requires `Vara Acida` or `Vara Cristal`. `Coral` requires `Vara de Coral`. `Criogenico` requires `Criogenia`. `Prometido` requires `Promessa Luminescente`, `Ruina Prometida`, or `Azul Lâmina`. `Incinerado` requires `Magnasas`. Several missions and crafting recipes depend on one of these mutations appearing on a specific fish. If you are waiting on a mutation and it is rod-exclusive, no amount of fishing without the right rod will produce it.

## Stage 1: Settle In

### Level band

Roughly levels 1 to 4.

### Main goals

- Finish `Primeira Pesca`.
- Finish `Vendedor Iniciante`.
- Reach 60% of `Lagoa Tranquila`.
- Start building a small money cushion.

### What to do

The opening is simple on paper, but this is where the tone of the whole file gets set. Spend enough time in `Lagoa Tranquila` to become comfortable with the minigame. The best early habit is not "catch fast," but "catch clean." Faster and cleaner catches lead to better XP momentum because perfect catches matter.

`Lagoa Tranquila` has seven fish: Carpa, Curimata, Jundia, Lambari, Pintado, Pirarucu, and Tilapia. None of them are rare, but discovering all seven is your first real goal. The pool skews heavily toward common catches, so the rare and epic fish will take a few return visits even once you feel like you have seen most of what it has to offer.

`Mar Aberto` is already available, and that is easy to overlook. Do not treat it as later content. It is your first natural money break and your first reminder that rotating pools is healthy. `Mar Aberto` has a wider rarity spread than `Lagoa Tranquila` — Atum, Robalo, Garoupa, Anchova, Tucunare, Marlim Azul, and Sardinha are all here — and the fish sell for meaningfully more. It is the better pool for building early cash. If `Lagoa Tranquila` starts to feel stale, switch to `Mar Aberto`, sell a bit, then return.

`Primeira Pesca` only needs 3 catches. Complete it immediately. It unlocks `Vendedor Iniciante`, which only needs 3 sold fish. Complete that too, right away — it is easy to forget that `Vendedor Iniciante` is what actually starts the path toward `Pantano Mushgrove` and also toward `Baia do Sol`. It also hands you the `Vara Brilhante` unlock. These are two important mid-game chains that begin the moment you sell three fish in Stage 1.

Once `Primeira Pesca` and `Vendedor Iniciante` are done, your real short-term target is `Olho no Bestiário`, which asks for 60% of `Lagoa Tranquila`.

### Key bottlenecks

**Olho no Bestiário:** 60% of `Lagoa Tranquila`. No specific fish — just discovery.

**Vendedor Iniciante:** Sell 3 fish. This is the trigger for the `Pantano Mushgrove` and `Baia do Sol` mission chains, and for unlocking `Vara Brilhante` in the shop. Do not delay it.

### Money this stage

`Mar Aberto` is your best early earner. Sell freely there — you do not need to hoard common `Mar Aberto` fish except `Sardinha`. Start holding `Sardinha` now: you will deliver 50 of them to a character you meet after unlocking `Geleira Snowcap`, and catching 50 across many sessions is much easier than farming them all in one go later. Everything else from `Mar Aberto` can be sold without regret. Your goal by the end of this stage is a cash cushion somewhere between 500 and 1000, which gives you room to open both `Grande Recife` (400) and `Geleira Snowcap` (1200) once you hit level 5.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

Nothing rod-exclusive matters yet, and no crafting recipes are within reach this early. If you see a `Brilhante` fish, that mutation is available from the start with `Vara Brilhante` — but the rod costs 3250, which is steep at this stage. Do not force it. The mutation will have plenty of time to accumulate once the rod is affordable.

### Active hunts

No hunts fire in `Lagoa Tranquila` or `Mar Aberto` at this stage. You will encounter your first hunt in `Mar Aberto` once the chain is live, but that comes later.

### Rod and upgrade advice

The default rods are enough here, but if one feels significantly worse than another, swap. `Vara de Carbono` is available from the start at 2000 and is one of the most dependable early rods — high `kg_max` and solid `control` with a reasonable luck value. If you are finding catches awkward, buying it early is not a waste. Avoid upgrading default rods in this stage; that money is better saved for upcoming unlock costs.

### 100% mindset

This is one of the few places where a clean early 100% can genuinely make the run feel better rather than slower. `Lagoa Tranquila` is compact enough that finishing it early is often satisfying. If the last one or two fish are proving stubborn, move on and return — do not camp for completion if it means stalling the rest of your progression.

## Stage 2: River, Swamp, and First Branching

### Level band

Roughly levels 4 to 7.

### Main goals

- Unlock `Rio Correnteza`.
- Push `Rio Correnteza` to 80%.
- Unlock `Pantano Mushgrove`.
- Reach level 5 and spend 400 for `Grande Recife`.
- Reach level 5 and spend 1200 for `Geleira Snowcap`.
- Reach level 7 and spend 1500 for `Baia do Sol`.

### What to do

`Rio Correnteza` is your first real lesson in bestiary-based progression. It is not enough to merely visit it. The pool has nine fish — Arapaima, Dourado, Jau, Matrinxa, Pacu, Piracanjuba, Piranha, Surubim, and Traira — and you want 80% there before `Pantano Mushgrove` opens. That is a real threshold, not a suggestion. Keep it active until you reach it. Among these nine, `Surubim` is the most important one to remember and hold. You will need one `Surubim` carrying the `Brilhante` mutation much later to unlock `Caverna Luminosa`. It is a river fish that you will probably stop visiting well before that requirement becomes active, so banking extras now means you will not need to return to an early pool in the midgame just to find one. `Piracanjuba` is also worth holding — it is a crafting ingredient for the `Frenesis` rod.

At the same time, your level milestones start opening side branches, each with a money cost that many players miss the first time:

- Level 5 and 400 money opens `Grande Recife`.
- Level 5 and 1200 money opens `Geleira Snowcap`.
- Level 7 and 1500 money opens `Baia do Sol`.

Do not read this as "pick one." Open all three when possible, then rotate. But keep your wallet in mind — if you are spending freely on upgrades, you may arrive at level 5 without enough to open `Geleira Snowcap`, which delays a long chain.

`Pantano Mushgrove` should not be dismissed as a small side biome. It will matter all the way into the late game because it feeds the `Densa Mata` branch. The swamp has eight fish: Amia, Bruxeta, Caranguejo Pantanoso, Carpa Cinza, Fungo Vivo, Jacaré, Limo Saltador, and Vieira-Lama, plus the stealthy `Peixe-Mão`. `Vieira-Lama` is a common catch — it will fill your inventory constantly — but hold a few rather than selling all of them. You will eventually need one carrying the `Envenenado` mutation for the `Densa Mata` chain.

`Geleira Snowcap` is a good pace-break area and a genuine early revenue pool. Opening it also unlocks the penguin side missions and the `Celestia` unlock chain, so it is not just a detour. `Baia do Sol` feels modest at first but later becomes structurally important and launches the `Fossa das Marianas` chain. The sooner you open these, the more time their associated mission chains have to mature naturally.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock Pantano Mushgrove:** 80% of `Rio Correnteza`. No specific fish required — push discovery.

**Unlock Grande Recife:** Level 5 and 400 money.

**Unlock Geleira Snowcap:** Level 5 and 1200 money. Unlocking it also starts the `Celestia` and penguin mission chains.

**Unlock Baia do Sol:** Level 7 and 1500 money.

### Money this stage

`Grande Recife` becomes your primary money pool once it opens. It has a better rarity spread than the early pools, and the Legendary fish there sell for meaningful amounts. `Geleira Snowcap` is also decent, particularly `Esturjão` and `Lúcio` which have solid base values. Sell freely from both of these — you are past the point where a 400-to-1200 unlock window is a real concern, and the next big money gate (Baia do Sol at 1500, and later Pantano + O Jardim) is manageable with normal rotation. Continue holding `Sardinha` from `Mar Aberto` until you have 50.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Criogenia` is the first craft that becomes available in this stage, unlocking when you open `Geleira Snowcap`. Its recipe needs 3 `Lúcio` (a common fish in the snowcap), 1 fish carrying the `Congelado` mutation (which appears naturally in cold-zone pools at a low base rate), and 3000 money. `Congelado` is not rod-exclusive — it appears randomly — but it is rare enough that you should watch for it actively while in `Geleira Snowcap`. If you find a `Congelado` catch, hold it immediately.

Start using `Vara Brilhante` in pools where `Brilhante` accumulation matters. The rod unlocked from the shop after `Vendedor Iniciante`, and even light use in `Grande Recife` or `Rio Correnteza` will start building toward later requirements. `Surubim` with `Brilhante` is the one that matters most — whenever you are in `Rio Correnteza` with `Vara Brilhante` equipped, that is a useful catch to keep.

### Active hunts

`Ataque a Lula!` fires in `Mar Aberto`. It does not become available until after the mission chain is live, but once it appears, participate. The boss fish `Lula Gigante` is a Mythic catch and the first ingredient in the `Vara do Kraken` crafting recipe. That recipe also needs `Marlim Azul` (from `Mar Aberto`) and 5 `Vieira-Lama` (from `Pantano Mushgrove`). You do not need the rod urgently, but catching the `Lula Gigante` when the hunt fires costs nothing extra and saves a dedicated farming trip later. Hold every copy you catch.

### Rod and upgrade advice

`Vara de Coral` costs 950 and is needed to produce the `Coral` mutation, which you will eventually need 5 of for the `Vulcao do Sol` unlock. Buy it early and keep it equipped in `Baia do Sol` and `Grande Recife` so `Coral` catches accumulate naturally over time. Waiting to buy it until the requirement is urgent means farming those 5 mutations in one session instead of collecting them passively.

`Vara Brilhante` at 3250 is a mid-range investment. If the money is comfortable, buy it now. If cash is tight, prioritize the pool unlock costs first. Once you have it, keep it rotating into pools where `Brilhante` targets matter.

Do not upgrade yet unless a specific catch is failing repeatedly. The unlock costs in this stage are the priority.

### 100% mindset

`Rio Correnteza` is a strong candidate for an early natural 100%. `Pantano Mushgrove` does not need to be forced yet, but it should stay active in your route instead of being forgotten.

## Stage 3: Reef Progress and the Garden Pivot

### Level band

Roughly levels 5 to 9.

### Main goals

- Build serious progress in `Grande Recife`.
- Push `Pantano Mushgrove` to 50%.
- Unlock `O Jardim`.
- Start the `Densa Mata` chain.
- Begin treating crafting as part of progression, not optional extra content.

### What to do

This is the point where the run widens. `Grande Recife` is one of the most important midgame pools because it leads deeper into the ocean route. The reef has thirteen fish: Peixe Gaio Coral, Baiacu do Recife, Batata do Mar, Borboletim, Coral Vivo, Goraz Flamejante, Imperador do Coral, Labro Pintado, Marimba, Pedra, Raia Coroa, Sardinha Coral, and Tetra-Serpae. Two of these deserve extra attention. `Imperador do Coral` is a Legendary fish — you need to catch one to unlock `Profundezas Desoladas`. Hold every copy you find, and if you hit 80% of the pool before seeing one, keep fishing there. The unlock requires the catch, not the delivery, so it just needs to hit your line once. `Tetra-Serpae` is a Rare fish needed in triplicate for the `Trinity` crafting recipe, with each copy requiring a different mutation. You will not be building Trinity this early since the Espiral variant requires the `Vertigem` rod from a later stage, but start holding any `Tetra-Serpae` you catch. The Noir and Albino variants are from random natural rolls on any fish, so they could appear at any point. The Espiral copy will need to wait for `Vertigem`, but having the other two already banked means the craft will come together quickly when that rod is available. `Pedra` is also worth noting — it is a Common reef fish that you will eventually need 20 of with the `Brilhante` mutation for the `Sniper` tribute recipe. That is a long way off, but using `Vara Brilhante` occasionally in the reef now means you will not have to deliberately grind it later.

`Pantano Mushgrove` leads in a very different direction, and `O Jardim` is where that direction becomes clearly substantial. Open `O Jardim` as soon as you can — it costs 50% of `Pantano Mushgrove` and 1000 money at the unlock prompt. Once it is available, split your sessions between:

- `Grande Recife` for main-route momentum
- `Pantano Mushgrove` for swamp progression
- `O Jardim` for long-term side progression and crafts

`O Jardim` has ten fish: Aurumim, Borboleta d'Água, Carpa Rosa, Enguia Trepadeira, Koi Ancestral, Lírio Vivente, Peixe Folha, Peixe Orquídea, Sapo Verde, and Tartaruga Musgo. Explore them steadily rather than rushing for completion.

When both `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` reach 50%, `Densa Mata: Prologo` opens. That does not mean the branch is ready to be finished. It means the game wants you to start respecting it as a long-term project. The next step after the prologue costs poison-based mutations and a specific fish from the swamp, so keeping `Pantano Mushgrove` active is not optional if you care about completing this branch.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock O Jardim:** 50% of `Pantano Mushgrove` and 1000 money.

**Densa Mata: Prologo:** 50% of `Pantano Mushgrove` and 50% of `O Jardim`. Both need to move together.

**Densa Mata Part 1 (preview):** After the prologue, the next step asks for 4 fish with the `Envenenado` mutation and 1 `Vieira-Lama` carrying `Envenenado`. Both come from `Pantano Mushgrove`. `Envenenado` is rod-exclusive — it only appears with `Vara Acida` or `Vara Cristal` equipped. `Vara Acida` costs 4000 and is available in the shop. Buy it before you start actively working the swamp for this mission. At 40% chance per catch, the mutation accumulates quickly once you have the right rod.

**Unlock Profundezas Desoladas (preview):** 80% of `Grande Recife` plus catching 1 `Imperador do Coral`, which is a Legendary fish in `Grande Recife`. Hold every copy you catch.

### Money this stage

`Grande Recife` and `O Jardim` are both solid earners now. The reef produces a steady stream of uncommon and rare fish with decent base values, while `O Jardim` has higher-value naturals once you start seeing its rarer fish. `Geleira Snowcap` also continues to produce strong returns when you visit. At this stage your spend priority is the `O Jardim` unlock (1000) and the `Vara Acida` if you are ready to start the poison chain (4000). Everything beyond that can be reinvested into rod upgrades or held for the upcoming `Baia do Sol` and `Vulcao do Sol` chains.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

Opening `O Jardim` activates most of the crafting menu. Several recipes unlock here, though many of their ingredients require later pools:

`Trinity` needs three `Tetra-Serpae` — one with Noir, one with Albino, and one with Espiral. The Espiral variant requires the `Vertigem` rod, which is not available until Stage 5. Bank the other two variants now and wait.

`Frenesis` needs 1 `Artefato-Espiral` (from `Vertigo`, Stage 5) and 1 `Piracanjuba` (from `Rio Correnteza`). Hold any `Piracanjuba` you still have from the river.

`Criogenia` (unlocked at `Geleira Snowcap`) is the most buildable craft right now. If you have the `Lúcio` and a `Congelado` catch and 3000 money, build it. It is a useful cold-zone rod that also directly helps with the `Criogenico` mutation delivery in the Marianas chain later.

`Envenenado` accumulation starts once you have `Vara Acida`. Keep it equipped in `Pantano Mushgrove` whenever you are working swamp content. The mutation appears on any fish in the pool, including `Vieira-Lama`.

### Active hunts

`Ataque a Lula!` continues to fire in `Mar Aberto`. Clear it whenever it appears if you are still visiting `Mar Aberto` for `Sardinha` or bestiary progress. `Coroa de Espinhos` fires in `Grande Recife`. Participate whenever it appears — the boss fish `Coroa de Espinhos` is a Legendary catch that takes the hunt's name, and clearing it while you are already working the reef costs nothing extra.

### Rod and upgrade advice

Buy `Vara Acida` (4000) before you plan to seriously advance the `Densa Mata` chain. It is also a decent all-around rod for the poison route and will make the swamp much more productive.

`Vara de Coral` should be your active rod in `Baia do Sol` whenever you are visiting. The `Coral` mutation you are building toward (5 catches needed for `Vulcao do Sol`) accumulates passively while you work bestiary progress there.

If `Grande Recife` or `Pantano Mushgrove` is generating difficult catches, `control` is the upgrade to reach for. Both pools have fish with short reaction windows at Rare and above.

### Why this stage matters

This is the stage that decides whether your file becomes smooth or cluttered later. Players who only rush the next ocean unlock tend to arrive in the endgame with the swamp and garden still mostly untouched. That always costs more time than keeping them warm now.

### 100% mindset

Try to leave this phase with `Grande Recife` and the swamp-garden pair all in healthy progress. You do not need every one of them at 100%, but you do want them to feel established rather than ignored.

## Stage 4: Desert and Sun-Coast Setup

### Level band

Roughly levels 9 to 12.

### Main goals

- Reach level 9 and unlock `Deserto Taara`.
- Open `A Fonte`.
- Push `Baia do Sol` toward `Vulcao do Sol`.
- Keep `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` active enough that `Densa Mata` does not fall behind.

### What to do

`Deserto Taara` opens at level 9, but only after delivering 1 fish carrying the `Arenoso` mutation. `Arenoso` is a natural mutation — it is not rod-exclusive — but it only appears on fish in desert-adjacent zones. If you have been rotating through the broader game, you may not have seen it yet. Keep an eye on catches from sandy or arid-flavored pools as you approach level 9. The desert has ten fish: Ancião de Areia, Areia, Aruana Sepultado, Bagre Sedimentar, Escorpião de Âmbar, Fóssil Errante, Piramboia Taara, Saurio das Dunas, Serpente Dunária, and Xeique de Taara. Among these, `Xeique de Taara` is a Legendary fish with a short reaction window — it demands the most from your rod and reflexes. Hold every copy. It is needed to unlock the `Ouro Fervente` rod.

Once you reach the desert, do not expect it to behave like a quick side zone. Give it enough time to establish itself, then work toward `A Fonte`. That unlock asks for 70% of `Deserto Taara` and 2 more delivered `Arenoso` catches, so the clean way to do it is to keep fishing the desert until both the bestiary and the mutation count are moving together.

`A Fonte` has ten fish of its own and expands both the bestiary and your money options in this region. It is also home to `Espirito de Taara`, a Legendary fish that will eventually matter for the area's full completion. Start familiarizing yourself with the pool even if you do not plan to finish it right away.

`Baia do Sol` should keep moving. The full chain toward `Vulcao do Sol` and then the `Fossa das Marianas` is longer than it looks. The bay has nine fish: Arraia Manta, Bagre Limpa-Vidro, Baiacu Caixa Amarelo, Borboleta do Mar, Enguia de Fita, Peixe-Palhaço, Perca, Polvo Dumbo, and Sardinha da Baía. `Peixe-Palhaço` deserves special attention here — the second penguin mission (`Pinguim... de novo?`) asks you to deliver 25 of them. If you are already working `Baia do Sol` for bestiary progress, hold `Peixe-Palhaço` as you go rather than selling them. The mission reward unlocks the `Vara da Amizade` crafting recipe. There is a critical dependency at the end of this chain: the `Fossa das Marianas` prologue requires a `Banshee Ígnea`, which is a Legendary fish found only in `Vulcao do Sol`. You cannot begin the Marianas chain at all until `Vulcao do Sol` is unlocked and you have caught one. Do not let the sun-coast branch sit idle.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock Deserto Taara:** Level 9 and 1 fish with `Arenoso` mutation delivered.

**Unlock A Fonte:** 70% of `Deserto Taara` and 2 delivered `Arenoso` fish. Fish the desert steadily and both conditions move together.

**Calor que Forja (Ouro Fervente rod):** Requires 1 `Xeique de Taara` delivered, 1 `Serpente Dunária` carrying `Incinerado` delivered, and 5000 total money earned. `Xeique de Taara` is a Legendary desert fish. `Incinerado` is rod-exclusive and only appears with `Magnasas` equipped — that rod requires late-game ingredients to craft. Do not treat this as an urgent mission, but keep `Xeique de Taara` copies when you find them.

**Unlock Vulcao do Sol:** 75% of `Baia do Sol`, level 12, and 5 fish carrying the `Coral` mutation caught. `Coral` is rod-exclusive and only appears with `Vara de Coral` equipped. If you bought that rod in Stage 2 and have been using it, these catches should be accumulating. If not, equip `Vara de Coral` in `Baia do Sol` now and stay until you have 5.

**Fossa das Marianas Prologue (critical dependency):** This mission costs 2000 money and requires delivering 1 `Banshee Ígnea`. The `Banshee Ígnea` is a Legendary fish found only in `Vulcao do Sol`. You cannot begin the Marianas chain until `Vulcao do Sol` is unlocked and you have caught one. Keep this in mind when you are deciding how much time to spend on the sun-coast branch.

### Money this stage

`Deserto Taara` and `A Fonte` both produce higher base values than most early pools, particularly at Rare and Legendary tiers. `Xeique de Taara` at 165 base value is one of the better earners in this level band. `Vulcao do Sol`, once unlocked, has strong fish across all rarity tiers — `Salmão de Obsidiana`, `Espadarte de Obsidiana`, and `Banshee Ígnea` all carry good base values. The Marianas prologue costs 2000, so do not arrive at `Vulcao do Sol` without a cash cushion. Sell freely from `Baia do Sol` except for `Peixe-Palhaço` and `Coral` mutation fish you still need.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Coral` catches (5 needed for `Vulcao do Sol`) should be your active mutation project in `Baia do Sol`. Use `Vara de Coral` there until you have met the threshold.

`Envenenado` continues to accumulate in `Pantano Mushgrove` with `Vara Acida`. Keep working it. You will need 10 total `Envenenado` fish for the Marianas chain later, and 4 plus a specific `Vieira-Lama` for the `Densa Mata` mission before that.

`Arenoso` appears naturally in the desert. Two delivered copies unlock `A Fonte`. Beyond that, it has no major recipe or mission uses.

The `Magnasas` recipe unlocks at `O Jardim`, but its ingredients include `Salmão de Obsidiana` (from `Vulcao do Sol`) and `Peixe Ninja` (from `As profundezas`, which is very late game). This craft will not be buildable for a long time regardless of when the recipe appears in your menu.

### Active hunts

No new hunts fire in the desert or bay pools. `Coroa de Espinhos` in `Grande Recife` and `Ataque a Lula!` in `Mar Aberto` may still be worth clearing if they appear during other sessions.

### Rod and upgrade advice

If your `Baia do Sol` catches are feeling tight, `Vara de Coral` has good `control` and will smooth out the sessions there. `Vara Acida` is the workhorse for the swamp. If you built `Criogenia` in Stage 2 and are visiting `Geleira Snowcap`, it handles the cold zone cleanly. Hold cash for the `Vulcao do Sol` progression — the Marianas prologue cost (2000) comes up quickly after you open the volcanic pool.

### 100% mindset

`Deserto Taara` and `A Fonte` are both fine to leave short of 100% for now if the final catches are slowing you down. The important thing is that they should not remain untouched side branches forever. `Baia do Sol` should be in active progress and heading toward the 75% threshold.

## Stage 5: Vertigo, Deep Reef, and the Midgame Wall

### Level band

Roughly levels 10 to 15.

### Main goals

- Push `Mar Aberto` to 80% and reach level 10 to unlock `Vertigo`.
- Push `Grande Recife` to 80% and catch `Imperador do Coral` to unlock `Profundezas Desoladas`.
- Pick up `Vertigem` if it matches your playstyle.
- Use hunts and side routes to break up the grind.

### What to do

This is the first truly important parallel stage. You are advancing two major routes at the same time:

- `Mar Aberto` into `Vertigo`
- `Grande Recife` into `Profundezas Desoladas`

Both matter. `Vertigo` looks like a side branch at first, but it later gates `As profundezas` — and `As profundezas` gates `Angra Cristal`. The pool has ten fish: Artefato-Espiral, Barracunfunda, Enguia, Girondina, Pato-Borracha, Peixe Aranha, Polvo Luminoso, Rodaval, Tontolume, and Vertana. Of these, `Artefato-Espiral`, `Barracunfunda`, and `Polvo Luminoso` are the most important to hold. `Artefato-Espiral` is a crafting ingredient for `Frenesis`. `Polvo Luminoso` (2 copies) and `Pato-Borracha` (1 copy) together unlock the `Vertigem` rod from a mission here. And `Barracunfunda` is the fish you will need for the `As profundezas` unlock — two copies carrying `Brilhante`.

`Profundezas Desoladas` is even more central because it fans out into multiple unlocks from a single pool:

- 60% opens `Piscina de Salmoura`
- 70% plus a bright `Surubim` opens `Caverna Luminosa`
- A separate mission chain unlocks `Tridente`

Your goal is not to visit the deep zone once. Your goal is to establish it as one of your core active pools and keep it moving across multiple sessions.

This is also the time to note a future wall that needs early preparation: `As profundezas` requires level 18, two `Barracunfunda` with `Brilhante`, and one `Artefato-Espiral`. All three fish come from `Vertigo`. The `Brilhante` mutation needs `Vara Brilhante` equipped to appear efficiently. Start looking for those fish in `Vertigo` with the right rod now so they are not a surprise later.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock Vertigo:** 80% of `Mar Aberto` and level 10. Both must be met simultaneously.

**Vertigem rod:** Delivering 2 `Polvo Luminoso` and 1 `Pato-Borracha` — all three found in `Vertigo`. Unlock the rod here if the playstyle suits you.

**Unlock Profundezas Desoladas:** 80% of `Grande Recife` and 1 `Imperador do Coral` caught. `Imperador do Coral` is a Legendary fish with a fast reaction window. Keep every copy.

**Unlock Piscina de Salmoura:** 60% of `Profundezas Desoladas`. Follows naturally from working the deep pool.

**Unlock Caverna Luminosa:** 70% of `Profundezas Desoladas` and 1 `Surubim` carrying `Brilhante`. `Surubim` is a `Rio Correnteza` fish. If your river inventory is empty and you have moved past `Rio Correnteza`, you will need to return. This is why keeping extras from early pools matters.

**Tridente rod:** Delivering 1 `Madeira Lunar` (a Legendary in `Geleira Snowcap`), 2 `Fantasma de Salmoura` (from `Piscina de Salmoura`), and 1 fish with the `Elétrico` mutation. `Elétrico` appears naturally at a low base rate — keep any `Elétrico` catch you find. The `Tridente` is a strong late rod worth planning around.

**As profundezas (future wall — start banking now):** Level 18, 2 `Barracunfunda` with `Brilhante`, and 1 `Artefato-Espiral`. All three fish come from `Vertigo`. Use `Vara Brilhante` there intentionally.

### Money this stage

`Profundezas Desoladas` has a notably better rarity spread than the early pools, with decent Legendary and Mythic rates. Sell fish there freely except anything tied to mutations you are working or mission deliveries you anticipate. `Vertigo` is similarly productive. `Geleira Snowcap` remains strong for `Madeira Lunar` value (Legendary at 120 base).

The `Promessa Luminescente` rod costs 4200 and becomes available when `Caverna Luminosa` opens. Budget for it — it is the primary tool for the `Prometido` mutation project and makes the cave branch much more productive.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Brilhante` is your active priority in `Vertigo` now. Use `Vara Brilhante` specifically for `Barracunfunda` targets.

`Espiral` from `Vertigem` becomes available once you unlock that rod. Equip it in `Vertigo` when you are actively fishing there. The `Espiral` `Tetra-Serpae` for `Trinity` can now be acquired — return to `Grande Recife` with `Vertigem` equipped for a session to finish that set.

`Envenenado` count keeps building in `Pantano Mushgrove`. You need 10 total `Envenenado` for Marianas Parte 3, and 4 plus a `Vieira-Lama (Envenenado)` for `Densa Mata Parte 1`. Both are accumulating if `Vara Acida` is in your rotation.

If you have the `Artefato-Espiral` and a `Piracanjuba` from `Rio Correnteza`, `Frenesis` is now buildable.

If you have the three `Tetra-Serpae` variants, `Trinity` is now buildable (costs 5000).

### Active hunts

`Eletricidade Desolada` fires in `Profundezas Desoladas`. The boss fish is `Água-Viva Imperatriz`, a Mythic catch that is the primary ingredient in both the `Sniper` tribute recipe and the `Perforatio` crafting recipe (which needs it with a `Solar` mutation — very rare without `Azul Lâmina`). Even if you are not chasing either recipe now, catching the boss during the hunt is nearly free since you will be in the pool anyway. Hold every `Água-Viva Imperatriz` you catch.

`O Chamado Azul` fires in `Caverna Luminosa` once that pool is open. The boss `Dragão Azul` is a Legendary fish needed to unlock `Caverna Carmesim` (it must carry the `Carmesim` mutation — work toward that before delivering) and it is the core ingredient in the `Azul Lâmina` tribute recipe. Participate whenever the hunt fires. Keep every copy — you will eventually need four of them for that recipe.

`Coroa de Espinhos` in `Grande Recife` may still fire if you are working toward 80% there.

### Rod and upgrade advice

Buy `Promessa Luminescente` (4200) as soon as `Caverna Luminosa` opens. The `Prometido` mutation is rod-exclusive to this rod, `Ruina Prometida`, and `Azul Lâmina`. Getting it early means mutation catches accumulate across your natural cave sessions rather than requiring a dedicated farming phase later.

`Vara Brilhante` should stay in your rotation specifically for `Vertigo` sessions. Do not equip it elsewhere unless you are actively farming a `Brilhante` requirement in another pool.

`Vertigem` is comfortable for long `Vertigo` sessions and produces `Espiral` passively. If the spiral vortex aesthetic and the slash mechanic suit your reflexes, it is a good choice for this region.

### 100% mindset

This is where many good files either become tidy or become messy. Midgame cleanup is extremely efficient. Pools in the 80 to 95 percent range should be taken seriously here. If `Mar Aberto`, `Grande Recife`, or `Vertigo` is already close to finished, close it now instead of postponing it.

## Stage 6: Caves, Sky, and Late-Midgame Breadth

### Level band

Roughly levels 14 to 18.

### Main goals

- Push `Profundezas Desoladas` to 70%.
- Unlock `Piscina de Salmoura`.
- Unlock `Caverna Luminosa`.
- Unlock `Caverna Carmesim`.
- Reach level 15 and unlock `Celestia`.
- Reach level 18 and unlock `As profundezas`.

### What to do

This is the broadest stage in the game, and it becomes much easier if you stop trying to finish one route before touching the others.

`Caverna Luminosa` has nine fish: Arraia Luminar, Carpa Etérea, Celacantozinho, Enguia Arcana, Enguia Pulsante, Lambari Luzente, Medusa do Breu, Peixe Farol, and Salmão do Breu. Among these, `Carpa Etérea` and `Peixe Farol` are needed in pairs (2 of each) to unlock `Celestia`. Bank them before level 15 arrives so the unlock is seamless. `Enguia Arcana` is an Epic fish worth holding for its value — do not discard it.

`Caverna Luminosa` then leads to `Caverna Carmesim`, but that unlock requires a `Dragão Azul` with the `Carmesim` mutation. `Carmesim` is a natural (non-rod-exclusive) mutation, but it is rare. The `Dragão Azul` comes only from the `O Chamado Azul` hunt in `Caverna Luminosa`. You must catch it during the hunt. Do not deliver it until it carries `Carmesim` — appraise it if needed once you have one, but hold it.

`Caverna Carmesim` has nine fish: Arraia Sangue, Barba Rubro, Caranguejo Inferno, Espadarte Carmesim, Lagosta Carmesim, Moray Escarlate, Piranha Brasa, Polvo Sangrenho, and Quimera Rubra. These fish are used in the `Ruina Prometida` crafting recipe alongside the `Dragão Carmesim` boss fish from `A Chama Carmesim` hunt.

At the same time:

- `Celestia` opens at level 15. It has sixteen fish including `Wyvern` and `Grifo Alado`, both needed for the `Fratura Celestial` crafting recipe. Start collecting those once the pool opens.
- `Piscina de Salmoura` opens at 60% of `Profundezas Desoladas`. The `Fantasma de Salmoura` here (2 copies needed) is required for the `Tridente` rod unlock. Fish the pool actively once it opens.
- `As profundezas` opens from `Vertigo` at level 18 with the specific fish requirements noted in Stage 5. Make sure those are in your inventory before level 18, not after.

That is a lot of surface area, which is exactly why rotation matters here. You should have at least four pools active at all times in this stage.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock Caverna Luminosa:** 70% of `Profundezas Desoladas` and 1 `Surubim` carrying `Brilhante` (from `Rio Correnteza`).

**Unlock Caverna Carmesim:** 70% of `Caverna Luminosa` and 1 `Dragão Azul` carrying `Carmesim`. The `Dragão Azul` is the boss of `O Chamado Azul` in `Caverna Luminosa`. You must catch it during the hunt. Apply `Carmesim` (naturally or by appraise) before delivering.

**Unlock Celestia:** Level 15, 2 `Carpa Etérea`, and 2 `Peixe Farol`. Both fish are in `Caverna Luminosa`. Bank them before level 15 so the unlock is automatic.

**Unlock As profundezas:** Level 18, 2 `Barracunfunda` with `Brilhante`, and 1 `Artefato-Espiral`. All from `Vertigo`. If you have been working that pool since Stage 5 with `Vara Brilhante`, these should be accumulating.

**Tridente rod:** 1 `Madeira Lunar` (from `Geleira Snowcap`), 2 `Fantasma de Salmoura` (from `Piscina de Salmoura`), 1 fish with `Elétrico` mutation. Pursue this once `Piscina de Salmoura` is active.

### Money this stage

`Caverna Luminosa` and `Caverna Carmesim` both have competitive base values at their Rare and above tiers. `Celestia` tends to have high-value fish across the board once you start seeing its Rare and Legendary slots. Sell freely from all three unless a fish is specifically needed for a mission, hunt delivery, or crafting. `Promessa Luminescente` (4200) should already be purchased. The next major gear investment is `Vara das Nuvens` (4500) if you are leaning into `Celestia`.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Prometido` is your active rod-exclusive project in `Caverna Luminosa` with `Promessa Luminescente`. Any fish carrying `Prometido` is worth holding if it is a quality catch. The `Ruina Prometida` recipe needs `Dragão Carmesim` (boss), 2 `Quimera Rubra`, 3 `Espadarte Carmesim`, and 9999 money — all from `Caverna Carmesim`. Start banking those ingredients once the crimson cave opens.

`Nuvem` mutation comes from `Vara das Nuvens` (or `Azul Lâmina` later). If you are working `Celestia` heavily, `Vara das Nuvens` makes it notably more productive and also contributes to the `Fratura Celestial` recipe, which needs 10 `Nuvem` mutation fish.

`Envenenado` keeps building. Aim to have at least 5 by the end of this stage. The 10-total requirement for Marianas Parte 3 is closer than it feels.

`Espiral` for `Trinity` is available if you have `Vertigem` and `Tetra-Serpae` available. Close that craft if the pieces are ready.

### Active hunts

`O Chamado Azul` fires in `Caverna Luminosa`. Clear it whenever it fires — you need `Dragão Azul` for the carmesim unlock and for the tribute recipe.

`A Chama Carmesim` fires in `Caverna Carmesim`. The boss `Dragão Carmesim` is needed for the `Ruina Prometida` crafting recipe. Attend whenever it appears.

`O Último Mito` also fires in `Caverna Carmesim`, but it is rare with a 30-minute cooldown and Mythic-only rewards. Treat it as a bonus when it fires.

`Eletricidade Desolada` in `Profundezas Desoladas` may still be active if you are pushing that pool to 70%.

### Rod and upgrade advice

`Promessa Luminescente` should be your cave rod. Its `Prometido` mutation rate makes cave sessions significantly more rewarding than using a neutral rod there.

`Vara das Nuvens` (4500) is worth buying if `Celestia` is part of your active rotation. It makes the sky branch smoother and helps the `Fratura Celestial` recipe ingredient count.

`Ruina Prometida` (the craft, not purchase) becomes available after `Caverna Carmesim` opens and the recipe unlocks. It is a meaningful upgrade once your cave progression supports building it.

If any single rod is still handling more than two zones comfortably, upgrade its `control` or `luck` before replacing it. A trusted rod with better stats beats an unfamiliar new one.

### 100% mindset

It is completely fine to have `Celestia`, `Caverna Luminosa`, and `Caverna Carmesim` all sitting below full completion at the same time. What matters is that they are progressing together and not being ignored.

## Stage 7: Mariana Staircase

### Level band

Late game into early endgame.

### Main goals

- Open `Rachadura Vulcanica` through the Marianas prologue.
- Advance through `Zona Glacial`, `Zenite Abissal`, and `Aguas Calmas`.
- Complete the sub-zone 100% requirements that unlock `Ponto Zero`.

### What to do

The Marianas route is best understood as a staircase. Each new pool is both a reward and a readiness check for the next one. You cannot rush through it — each step requires fish from the pool you just finished, so the work is inherently sequential.

The healthy way to play this branch is:

1. Unlock the next step.
2. Fish the new sub-zone until it starts giving useful familiarity and resources.
3. Step out to handle another branch, craft, hunt, or cleanup goal.
4. Return once the next requirement is realistic.

The biggest thing to understand is that this branch contains mandatory 100% progression. `Fossa das Marianas: O Fundo` requires 100% of `Rachadura Vulcanica`, `Zona Glacial`, `Zenite Abissal`, and `Aguas Calmas` — plus 12000 money spent. That is not optional prestige. That is the gate to `Ponto Zero`. Finish these sub-zones while they are tied directly to forward motion, not as postgame cleanup.

`Rachadura Vulcanica` has ten fish: Arraia Fuliginosa, Baiacu de Pirita, Blenio das Bolhas, Couro-de-Magma, Eglefino Infernal, Enguia das Brasas, Gladio Escaldante, Iguana Marinha Abissal, Leviata de Magma, and Moreia de Fusão. `Leviata de Magma` is a Legendary-tier fish needed for Marianas Parte 4. `Couro-de-Magma` and `Blenio das Bolhas` are needed for Parte 1 deliveries. `Gladio Escaldante` with `Carmesim` mutation is needed for the `Retribuição` tribute recipe — hold any `Gladio Escaldante` you catch and let `Carmesim` appear naturally over time.

`Zona Glacial` has ten fish including `Leviata Congelado` (needed for Marianas Parte 4 and the `Serenidade` tribute recipe), `Peixe-Vidro` and `Predador do Ártico` (needed for Parte 2), and `Quimera de Cristal`. Work it to 100%.

`Zenite Abissal` has ten fish including `Ânguila Coroada` (needed for Parte 4), `Diabo-Marinho Ancestral` (needed for Parte 3), and several that sell for strong values. The `Diabo-Marinho Ancestral` is a deeper-tier fish — keep every copy while building toward Parte 3.

`Aguas Calmas` has nine fish including `Disco de Diamante` (needed for the `Serenidade` tribute recipe with `Sereno` mutation). This pool tends to be the calmest of the four staircase zones to complete and has strong fish values throughout.

### Key bottlenecks

**Marianas Prologue:** 2000 money spent and 1 `Banshee Ígnea` delivered. `Banshee Ígnea` is a Legendary fish from `Vulcao do Sol`. Enter this stage with both in hand.

**Marianas Parte 1 (unlock Zona Glacial):** 1 `Couro-de-Magma` and 1 `Blenio das Bolhas` delivered. Both are in `Rachadura Vulcanica`. Fish it properly before assuming you can move on.

**Marianas Parte 2 (unlock Zenite Abissal):** 1 `Peixe-Vidro`, 1 `Predador do Ártico`, and 1 fish with `Criogenico` mutation delivered. Both named fish are in `Zona Glacial`. `Criogenico` is rod-exclusive to `Criogenia` at 15% chance. If you built `Criogenia` in Stage 2, equip it while working `Zona Glacial` and the mutation will accumulate naturally.

**Marianas Parte 3 (unlock Imminens Virga rod):** 1 `Diabo-Marinho Ancestral` from `Zenite Abissal` and 10 fish with `Envenenado` delivered. The Envenenado count is the heavier ask. If you have been running poison content since the swamp, this should be partially met already. If not, both `Zenite Abissal` and `Rachadura Vulcanica` produce fish you can apply `Envenenado` to with `Vara Acida`. The `Imminens Virga` is a powerful late rod unlocked as the reward.

**Marianas Parte 4 (unlock Aguas Calmas):** 1 `Leviata de Magma` from `Rachadura Vulcanica`, 1 `Leviata Congelado` from `Zona Glacial`, and 1 `Ânguila Coroada` from `Zenite Abissal`. All three are rare catches in their zones. Hold every copy across the entire staircase — these are not farmable from a single pool.

**Marianas Parte 5 — O Fundo (unlock Ponto Zero):** 100% of `Rachadura Vulcanica`, `Zona Glacial`, `Zenite Abissal`, and `Aguas Calmas`, plus 12000 money spent. Arrive prepared on both fronts.

### Money this stage

The Marianas sub-zones have some of the best fish values in the game. `Zenite Abissal` in particular produces several high-value Legendary and Mythic-tier fish. Sell freely except for specific delivery targets and tribute recipe ingredients. The 12000 spend requirement for Parte 5 is significant — do not burn through everything on upgrades before it is met. `Aguas Calmas` also tends to sell well and is one of the fastest pools to generate income during late-game cleanup sessions.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Criogenico` with `Criogenia` in `Zona Glacial` feeds the Parte 2 delivery requirement. Keep the rod equipped there.

`Envenenado` with `Vara Acida` anywhere in the Marianas staircase will advance toward the Parte 3 total. `Rachadura Vulcanica` produces active fish that carry `Envenenado` well.

`Incinerado` only appears with `Magnasas` equipped. If you have built or are building `Magnasas` by this point, use it in `Rachadura Vulcanica` to produce `Incinerado` candidates — this advances the `Calor que Forja` mission if that is still open.

`Sereno` will eventually matter for the `Serenidade` tribute recipe. It only appears with `Hollow Dusk` or `Serenidade` itself equipped. `Hollow Dusk` (3000) is available in the shop but only unlocks with `Cafeteria` access — so `Sereno` accumulation has to wait until the hidden pool is open. Just be aware it will come up.

### Rod and upgrade advice

`Ignis` (8000) makes `Rachadura Vulcanica` cleaner and has a solid Recover stat for the hot zone's aggressive fish. It also unlocks the pool — check if you need to own it before entry.

`Criogenia` is essential for the `Criogenico` delivery in Parte 2. If you have not already built it, do so before working `Zona Glacial` heavily.

`Zenite` (12000) is a strong stable late-game rod that works well across all four staircase zones. Its `alter` property gives you more time on each catch, which is valuable in the deep Marianas pools where fish are heavier and sequences are longer.

`Imminens Virga` (unlocked as the Parte 3 reward) is one of the best rods in the game. Equip it immediately and trust it through the remainder of the staircase.

### 100% mindset

This is one of the only parts of the game where "I will do the completion later" is usually the wrong answer. Finish these sub-zones while they are still tied directly to forward motion.

## Stage 8: Endgame Branch Closure

### Level band

Endgame cleanup and consolidation.

### Main goals

- Unlock `Ponto Zero`.
- Finish `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` to 100%.
- Finish the `Densa Mata` branch.
- Unlock `Templo de Micelio`.
- Close missing crafts, hunts, and mutation projects.

### What to do

This is where earlier discipline pays off. If you kept the swamp and garden in rotation, the `Densa Mata` line now feels like a delayed payoff. If you ignored them, it feels like homework.

The branch structure matters:

- 50% in both `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` starts the line (Prologo)
- Prologo leads to a poison delivery step (Parte 1)
- After that, the next mission asks for 45 minutes of play time and then checks for 100% in both pools (Parte 2)
- After both pools are at 100%, the final step requires 3 fish carrying the `Arcana` mutation, which unlocks `Templo de Micelio` (Parte 3)

The 45-minute timer means this branch rewards steady parallel play more than hard forcing. If you have been rotating through the run naturally, the timer is usually a non-issue. If you have been ignoring the swamp branch entirely, you may need to actively sit with it for a session.

The `Arcana` mutation is rod-exclusive and rare. It requires a specific rod to produce and does not appear through normal fishing without it. Identify the rod that produces `Arcana` and use it intentionally in the pools most likely to generate candidates. Plan for this as a targeted project rather than a lucky accident.

`Templo de Micelio` itself has nine fish: Carpa do Templo, Cascudo Ritual, Coroa do Santuário, Enguia Rúnica, Guarda Raiz Ancestral, Musgo Fantasma, Perca Vinhedo, Raia Micelial, and Serpe de Samambaia. Work it actively once it opens.

This is also a good moment to reevaluate every unfinished system:

- hunts you never finished
- rods you almost crafted
- rods you use a lot but never upgraded
- mission chains blocked by one missing fish or mutation

### Key bottlenecks

**Densa Mata Parte 1:** 4 fish with `Envenenado` and 1 `Vieira-Lama` carrying `Envenenado`. Both sources are in `Pantano Mushgrove`. Keep `Vara Acida` equipped while fishing swamp content.

**Densa Mata Parte 2:** 45 minutes of play time logged and 100% of both `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim`. The timer runs from the moment the mission is available, not from session start. Stay active.

**Densa Mata Parte 3 (unlock Templo de Micelio):** 3 fish with the `Arcana` mutation delivered. `Arcana` is rod-exclusive and rare. Identify which rod produces it and use it intentionally in the pools most likely to generate candidates.

**O Guardiao hunt:** Fires in `Templo de Micelio`. Clear it as part of your Templo de Micelio work.

### Money this stage

`Ponto Zero` has seven fish — Alevino de Éter, Andarilho das Nebulosas, Cascudo Estelar, Fragmento do Tempo, Relíquia da Singularidade, Sentinela do Vazio, and Sussurro do Horizonte — and they carry some of the highest base values in the game. Sell freely there. `Templo de Micelio` is also competitive at the Legendary tier. If money is tight for the 12000 Marianas Parte 5 spend, both of these pools will close that gap quickly.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

This is the natural closing point for most mid-tier crafts. If `Trinity`, `Frenesis`, or `Criogenia` is still open, finish them now. `Ruina Prometida` is buildable if `Caverna Carmesim` is in good shape. `Vara das Profundezas` becomes craftable once `As profundezas` is unlocked — its recipe needs `Dragão do Profundo` (the boss of `Escuridao Total...` hunt in `As profundezas`), 1 `Cobra do Mar` and 10 `Metal Enferrujado` (both from `As profundezas`), and 7777 money.

The `Fratura Celestial` recipe (needs 1 `Wyvern`, 2 `Grifo Alado`, 10 `Nuvem` fish, 7000 money) unlocks at 60% of `Celestia`. If `Celestia` is at or past that threshold, build it.

### Active hunts

`Encontro com o Maior` fires in `Ponto Zero`. The boss `Baleia Celeste` is a Mythic fish with one of the longest catch sequences in the game. Clear it while you are working `Ponto Zero`. `O Guardiao` fires in `Templo de Micelio`. `Escuridao Total...` fires in `As profundezas` — clear it to unlock the `Vara das Profundezas` recipe progress.

### Rod and upgrade advice

By this stage, your rod is probably chosen. If you are using `Imminens Virga`, lean into it across the late pools — its `slash` mechanic handles heavy fish well. If `Zenite` is your choice, it covers `Ponto Zero` and `Templo de Micelio` cleanly. Upgrade whichever rod you are using most — `control` if catches are tense, `luck` if you are still missing late discoveries.

### 100% mindset

By the end of this stage, most main-world pools should either be complete or so close that the end of the run is obvious.

## Stage 9: Final Core Completion

### Level band

Late endgame and prestige cleanup.

### Main goals

- Finish `Ponto Zero`.
- Reach level 20 and unlock `Angra Cristal`.
- Complete the remaining main-pool bestiaries.
- Claim global bestiary rewards.
- Decide whether to stop at core 100% or continue into hidden content.

### What to do

`Angra Cristal` is one of the last major main-world destinations and deserves focused time when it opens. Its unlock is strict enough that you should prepare well in advance: level 20, 50% of `As profundezas`, 5 `Salmajoia`, and 5 `Peixe Três-Olhos`. Both named fish are found in `As profundezas`. Start banking them the moment you first unlock that pool — if you arrive at level 20 with nothing stocked, you are looking at a deliberate farming session before the unlock is available.

`Angra Cristal` has five fish: Bagre Opalescente, Camarão Cristal, DJ Polvo Nopus, Lagosta Cristal, and Peixe Flauta. `Lagosta Cristal` is important to hold — it is needed with `Cristalizado` mutation for the `Retribuição` tribute recipe. `Cristalizado` is rod-exclusive to `Vara Cristal`, which also unlocks `Angra Cristal`. Equip `Vara Cristal` while you are here.

`Angra Cristal` also leads to hidden late-game content. The important spoiler-light advice is that thorough completion here matters and is rewarded.

By this point, your route should no longer be "what is the next area?" It should be "what is the best remaining piece of progress?"

The best cleanup order is usually:

1. Any pool that is one or two fish away from 100%.
2. Any mission chain blocked by one delivery or one mutation.
3. Any hunt you postponed.
4. Any craft tied to a rod or branch you still care about.
5. The last major pools that still need a full push.

### Key bottlenecks

**Unlock Angra Cristal:** Level 20, 5 `Salmajoia` delivered, 5 `Peixe Três-Olhos` delivered, and 50% of `As profundezas`. Both fish are found in `As profundezas`. Start collecting them the moment you unlock that pool.

**Escuridao Total...:** Fires in `As profundezas`. Clear it while working the pool. The boss `Dragão do Profundo` is needed for `Vara das Profundezas`.

**Encontro com o Maior:** Fires in `Ponto Zero`. Clear it as part of your Ponto Zero work.

**Fratura Celestial recipe:** Unlocks at 60% of `Celestia`. Needs 1 `Wyvern`, 2 `Grifo Alado`, 10 fish with `Nuvem` mutation, and 7000 money. Both fish are in `Celestia`. `Nuvem` requires `Vara das Nuvens` or `Azul Lâmina`. Close this craft when the pieces are ready.

**Vara da Amizade craft:** Unlocks from the `helpafriend2` mission chain (25 `Peixe-Palhaço` delivered, found in `Baia do Sol`). Needs 10 `Peixe-Corda` (from `As profundezas`) and 15 `Pedra` (from `Grande Recife`). Worth building once those ingredients are in hand.

### Money this stage

`Angra Cristal`, `Ponto Zero`, and `Templo de Micelio` are your strongest late earners. If you still need money for craft costs, tribute recipes, or the `Prisma` rod (25000 — which unlocks `Aguas Calmas`), rotate between these pools. `Angra Cristal` in particular tends to generate strong per-catch values even from its common fish tier.

### Mutations and crafts in progress

`Cristalizado` from `Vara Cristal` in `Angra Cristal` feeds the `Retribuição` recipe. Keep `Lagosta Cristal` with `Cristalizado` when you find one.

`Profundo` from `Vara das Profundezas` accumulates at 30% per catch. Use it in `As profundezas` for `Enguia Ância` targets — that fish with `Profundo` is another `Retribuição` ingredient.

`Peixe Três-Olhos` with `Espiral` mutation (from `Vertigem`) is also needed for `Retribuição`. Keep any `Peixe Três-Olhos` you find in `As profundezas` and use `Vertigem` there when you need to target the mutation.

### Suggestions

- Claim bestiary rewards as they come. They are part of progression now, not just trophies.
- If you are mentally done with a branch, finish it cleanly rather than letting it linger half-finished.
- When a pool is both rewarding money and still missing multiple rares, it is often a better session target than a nearly-empty checklist zone.

## Suggested Cleanup Order for a Full File

If you are nearing the end and are not sure what to finish first, this order usually keeps the file feeling good:

1. Main-route pools already above 90%.
2. Mariana sub-zones needed for `Ponto Zero`.
3. `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` if `Densa Mata` still needs them.

7. `Angra Cristal`.

## Rods Worth Planning Around

These are not all equally mandatory, but they tend to matter the most in a natural full-completion route:

- `Vara de Carbono` as a dependable early anchor
- `Vara de Coral` before `Baia do Sol` bestiary and `Vulcao do Sol` mutation requirements become urgent — the earlier you buy it, the more `Coral` catches accumulate passively
- `Vara Brilhante` before bright-fish progression walls — get this early enough that the `Brilhante` mutation has time to accumulate naturally
- `Vara Acida` before the `Densa Mata` poison chain and the Marianas Parte 3 `Envenenado` total become active walls
- `Vertigem` as a comfortable midgame pickup, unlocked by delivering fish from `Vertigo`, and the only efficient source of the `Espiral` mutation
- `Promessa Luminescente` once `Caverna Luminosa` opens — makes the `Prometido` mutation significantly more accessible and is the primary cave progression rod
- `Criogenia` before the cold branch demands `Criogenico` — build it at `Geleira Snowcap` for a smooth Marianas Parte 2
- `Ruina Prometida` as the cave branch payoff once `Caverna Carmesim` is established
- `Vara das Nuvens` if you spend serious time in `Celestia` and need `Nuvem` mutation progress
- `Ignis` before entering `Rachadura Vulcanica` seriously — the hot zone rewards its Recover stat
- `Zenite` across the whole Marianas staircase — the extended timing it provides is worth the price
- `Tridente` once you have `Madeira Lunar` from Snowcap and `Fantasma de Salmoura` from Brinepool
- `Imminens Virga` as the Marianas Parte 3 reward — equip it immediately
- `Vara das Profundezas` once the `Escuridao Total...` hunt and `As profundezas` are established
- `Vara Cristal` before `Angra Cristal` opens — it is the unlock requirement and also the only rod that produces `Cristalizado`

The best rod is not always the rod with the scariest numbers. A rod that matches your rhythm and lets you handle difficult fish cleanly is often worth more than a flashier one you do not trust.

## Money-Farming Reference

Money pressure in Fisching Overhaul follows the run's natural structure. The three biggest single costs are the `Marianas Parte 5` spend requirement (12000), the `Prisma` rod (25000 if you are pursuing `Aguas Calmas` through that route), and the tribute recipe costs — `Azul Lâmina` needs 15000, `Ruina Prometida` needs 9999, and `Retribuição`, `Sniper`, and others add up on top. Plan for those peaks rather than running tight and discovering them at the worst moment.

**Early game (Stages 1–3):** `Mar Aberto` is your best early earner. Its rarity spread is meaningfully better than `Lagoa Tranquila`. The seven non-Sardinha fish there sell for reasonable amounts, and the Rare and Epic slots are genuinely worth finding. Sell freely from `Mar Aberto` except `Sardinha`. Once `Grande Recife` opens, shift your primary selling to there — it combines better base values with the bestiary progress you need anyway.

**Mid game (Stages 4–6):** `Grande Recife`, `Deserto Taara`, `Vulcao do Sol`, and `Geleira Snowcap` are the best earners in this band. `Xeique de Taara` (Legendary, 165 base) is the best single-fish value available before the deep-ocean pools open. `Vulcao do Sol` has strong values across its entire roster and earns well per session once unlocked. `Caverna Luminosa` and `Caverna Carmesim` both produce high-value fish once you are working them for bestiary. Keep cash between 3000 and 5000 through this range to handle unlock costs and rod investments without pausing for a dedicated farm.

**Late game (Stages 7–9):** `Zenite Abissal` and `Aguas Calmas` are strong earners during the Marianas staircase. `Ponto Zero` has some of the highest-value fish in the entire game. `Angra Cristal` produces strong per-catch averages even at Common tier. Sell freely from all four of these. Do not burn through cash on upgrade spam just because the income feels high — the 12000 Marianas spend requirement, the tribute recipe costs, and any remaining rod purchases all pull from the same pool.

**When you need a targeted earn:** `Ponto Zero` and `Zenite Abissal` are the fastest options in the endgame. `Grande Recife` is the fastest option in the midgame. `Deserto Taara` is the fastest between Stages 4 and 5. In each case, aim for pools where your bestiary progress is still incomplete — simultaneous progress on two fronts is always better than fishing purely for gold.

## Crafting Recipe Reference

The crafting system has 17 recipes. Most unlock progressively as you open new pools, though several require ingredients from zones you have not yet reached when the recipe first appears in the menu. The recipe being available does not mean the craft is ready.

**Criogenia** unlocks when `Geleira Snowcap` opens. It needs 3 `Lúcio` (a common fish in the snowcap), 1 fish with the `Congelado` mutation (appears naturally at a low rate in cold-zone pools), and 3000 money. This is the most immediately buildable recipe after Stage 2. Build it as soon as the pieces arrive — `Criogenia` directly helps with the `Criogenico` delivery in Marianas Parte 2.

**Trinity** unlocks at `O Jardim`. It needs one `Tetra-Serpae` with `Noir`, one with `Albino`, and one with `Espiral`, plus 5000 money. `Tetra-Serpae` is a Rare fish in `Grande Recife`. `Noir` and `Albino` appear as natural random mutations and can be found on any `Tetra-Serpae` you catch there over time. `Espiral` requires the `Vertigem` rod, available in `Vertigo` (Stage 5). Bank the first two variants when you find them and wait for `Vertigem` to complete the set.

**Frenesis** unlocks at `O Jardim`. It needs 1 `Artefato-Espiral` from `Vertigo`, 1 `Piracanjuba` from `Rio Correnteza`, and 6000 money. Both fish are available well before the full recipe is ready. Hold any `Piracanjuba` from the river and any `Artefato-Espiral` from the spiral zone.

**Magnasas** unlocks at `O Jardim` but its ingredients come from much later zones. It needs 1 `Salmão de Obsidiana` carrying `Chamuscado` (from `Vulcao do Sol`) and 1 `Peixe Ninja` (from `As profundezas`), plus 4000 money. `Chamuscado` is a natural mutation that appears at a very low base rate. `Peixe Ninja` is not available until `As profundezas` opens (Stage 9 territory). This craft will sit in your menu for a long time before it is realistic.

**Midas** unlocks at `O Jardim`. It needs 6 `Salmajoia` each carrying `Cristalizado`. `Salmajoia` is found in `As profundezas` — very late game. `Cristalizado` only appears with `Vara Cristal`. Another recipe that is visible early but buildable only near the endgame.

**Perforatio** unlocks at `O Jardim`. It needs 1 `Água-Viva Imperatriz` carrying `Solar`, plus 5000 money. `Água-Viva Imperatriz` is the boss fish of `Eletricidade Desolada` in `Profundezas Desoladas`. `Solar` appears at an extremely low natural rate (under 0.2%). Without `Azul Lâmina` (which boosts it to 3%), this craft is very difficult to complete without patience or luck. Do not plan around it as a primary mid-game goal.

**Vara do Kraken** unlocks when you catch your first `Lula Gigante` — the boss of `Ataque a Lula!` in `Mar Aberto`. The recipe needs that same `Lula Gigante`, 1 `Marlim Azul` (from `Mar Aberto`), 5 `Vieira-Lama` (from `Pantano Mushgrove`), and 10000 money. If you catch the `Lula Gigante` during the hunt, the recipe immediately becomes available. All other ingredients are common; the only real obstacle is catching the hunt boss.

**Ruina Prometida** unlocks when `Caverna Carmesim` opens. It needs 1 `Dragão Carmesim` (boss of `A Chama Carmesim` in `Caverna Carmesim`), 2 `Quimera Rubra` (from `Caverna Carmesim`), 3 `Espadarte Carmesim` (from `Caverna Carmesim`), and 9999 money. The `Dragão Carmesim` only drops from the hunt, so clearing `A Chama Carmesim` is a prerequisite. `Quimera Rubra` and `Espadarte Carmesim` are Rare and Epic catches in the cave — they accumulate naturally from bestiary work.

**Fratura Celestial** unlocks at 60% of `Celestia`. It needs 1 `Wyvern` (Legendary in `Celestia`), 2 `Grifo Alado` (Rare in `Celestia`), 10 fish with `Nuvem` mutation, and 7000 money. `Nuvem` is rod-exclusive to `Vara das Nuvens` and `Azul Lâmina`. Work `Celestia` with `Vara das Nuvens` and the mutation count fills in alongside the bestiary.

**Vara das Profundezas** unlocks at `As profundezas`. It needs 1 `Dragão do Profundo` (boss of `Escuridao Total...` in `As profundezas`), 1 `Cobra do Mar` (from `As profundezas`), 10 `Metal Enferrujado` (also from `As profundezas`), and 7777 money. `Cobra do Mar` and `Metal Enferrujado` are common enough that they accumulate naturally from bestiary work. The hunt boss is the limiting ingredient.

**Vara da Amizade** unlocks after completing the `helpafriend2` mission (deliver 25 `Peixe-Palhaço` from `Baia do Sol`). It needs 10 `Peixe-Corda` (from `As profundezas`) and 15 `Pedra` (from `Grande Recife`). `Pedra` is common. `Peixe-Corda` accumulates from normal `As profundezas` sessions.

**Vara Eclipse** unlocks after catching 3 fish with the `Eclipsado` mutation. It needs 1 `Axolote da Meia Noite` (from `As profundezas`) and 1 fish with a `Solar` mutation, plus 8000 money. `Solar` is the hard ingredient again. The `Eclipsado` mutation itself requires `Vara Eclipse` to produce efficiently — meaning the unlock is the bottleneck, not the recipe once it opens.

The five **tribute recipes** — `Azul Lâmina`, `Serenidade`, `Retribuição`, `Sniper`, and `Mãos` — all unlock after `Cafeteria` is reached. They are covered in detail in the tribute rod section at the end of this guide.

## Mutation Reference

Mutations are divided into two categories in practice: natural mutations that appear on any fish with any rod at a fixed low rate, and rod-exclusive mutations that require a specific rod equipped to appear at all. The natural mutations — `Carmesim`, `Chamuscado`, `Congelado`, `Elétrico`, `Albino`, `Noir`, and `Arenoso` among others — can be collected passively over time and generally do not require planning. The rod-exclusive ones require intentional setup.

**Brilhante** — requires `Vara Brilhante`. Appears at 12.5% chance. The most broadly used mutation in the game, gating several unlock requirements (`Surubim` for `Caverna Luminosa`, `Barracunfunda` for `As profundezas`, and `Pedra` in large quantities for the `Sniper` tribute). Buy `Vara Brilhante` early and keep it rotating into whatever pool has an active `Brilhante` requirement. For `Pedra` with `Brilhante`, use it in `Grande Recife` during normal reef sessions.

**Envenenado** — requires `Vara Acida` or `Vara Cristal`. Appears at 40% chance. High frequency, but low gold value — `Envenenado` fish sell for only 30% of their base. Deliver them when missions ask; otherwise selling them is a last resort. Use `Vara Acida` in `Pantano Mushgrove` for the `Densa Mata` deliveries and in any deep zone for the Marianas Parte 3 total. Do not worry about the low value — the mission returns outweigh the sales loss.

**Coral** — requires `Vara de Coral`. Appears at 12% chance. Needed to catch 5 for the `Vulcao do Sol` unlock. Buy the rod early and rotate it into `Baia do Sol` so the count builds passively.

**Criogenico** — requires `Criogenia`. Appears at 15% chance. Needed for Marianas Parte 2 delivery. Build `Criogenia` early (available at `Geleira Snowcap`) and use it in `Zona Glacial` for a clean delivery.

**Espiral** — requires `Vertigem` or `Trinity`. Appears at 17.5% chance. Primary use is the `Tetra-Serpae (Espiral)` copy for the `Trinity` recipe. Use `Vertigem` in `Grande Recife` for a session to capture the mutation on `Tetra-Serpae`.

**Prometido** — requires `Promessa Luminescente`, `Ruina Prometida`, or `Azul Lâmina`. Appears at 10% base chance. Gated behind `Caverna Luminosa`. Buy `Promessa Luminescente` when the cave opens and use it there throughout your cave sessions. By the time you are ready to build `Azul Lâmina`, most of the hard `Prometido` farming should already be behind you.

**Incinerado** — requires `Magnasas`. Appears at 20% chance. Needed for the `Serpente Dunária (Incinerado)` delivery in the `Calor que Forja` mission and for the `Magnasas` recipe unlock logic. `Magnasas` itself is built from late-game ingredients, so `Incinerado` is a late-stage project. Use `Magnasas` in `Rachadura Vulcanica` when it is built for efficient production.

**Nuvem** — requires `Vara das Nuvens` or `Azul Lâmina`. Appears at 10% chance. Needed for `Fratura Celestial` (10 copies). Use `Vara das Nuvens` while working `Celestia` and the count fills in alongside bestiary progress.

**Cristalizado** — requires `Vara Cristal`. Appears at 20% chance. Needed for `Lagosta Cristal (Cristalizado)` in `Retribuição` and for the `Midas` recipe (6 `Salmajoia` with `Cristalizado`). Use `Vara Cristal` in `Angra Cristal` and `As profundezas`.

**Profundo** — requires `Vara das Profundezas`. Appears at 30% chance. Needed for `Enguia Ância (Profundo)` in `Retribuição`. Use the rod in `As profundezas` for efficient delivery.

**Sereno** — requires `Hollow Dusk` or `Serenidade`. Appears at 3% chance with `Hollow Dusk`, 30% with `Serenidade`. Needed for `Serenidade` tribute recipe (two `Peixe-Corda` and one `Disco de Diamante` each with `Sereno`). `Hollow Dusk` is only available after `Cafeteria` is unlocked. Once you have access, use it in `Profundezas Desoladas` for `Peixe-Corda` and in `Aguas Calmas` for `Disco de Diamante`.

**Solar** — appears naturally at under 0.2%. `Azul Lâmina` boosts it to 3%. Needed for `Perforatio` (with `Água-Viva Imperatriz`) and `Vara Eclipse` (any fish). The natural rate makes this one of the game's most time-consuming mutations to farm without the tribute rod. Both recipes that need it are endgame content — treat `Solar` as a long-term project rather than something to force early.

**Arcana** — rod-exclusive, rare. Required for `Densa Mata Parte 3` (3 fish). Identify which rod produces it and use it intentionally in the pools where the mutation is most likely to appear. `Pantano Mushgrove` and `O Jardim` are the natural candidates given the branch context.

## Hunt Reference

Hunts fire during regular fishing sessions in specific pools. Participation is never required mid-session, but the window is time-limited and the boss fish often cannot be obtained any other way. Participating whenever a hunt fires in a pool you are already working costs almost nothing extra.

**Ataque a Lula!** fires in `Mar Aberto`. The boss is `Lula Gigante`, a Mythic fish. Clearing this hunt also triggers the recipe unlock for `Vara do Kraken` — the recipe becomes available the moment you catch the boss. Beyond the crafting use, `Lula Gigante` is simply a massive high-value catch. Clear this hunt whenever `Mar Aberto` is in your rotation.

**Coroa de Espinhos** fires in `Grande Recife`. The boss is `Coroa de Espinhos`, a Legendary fish. It has no major crafting dependency, but the hunt fires frequently while you are working reef bestiary in Stages 3 and 5. Clear it as a pacing break. Its loot pool also carries improved Epic and Legendary rates, making reef sessions during an active hunt noticeably more rewarding.

**Eletricidade Desolada** fires in `Profundezas Desoladas`. The boss is `Água-Viva Imperatriz`, a Mythic fish and the primary ingredient for both `Sniper` (tribute recipe) and `Perforatio` (main recipe, requires `Solar` mutation variant). `Perforatio` is difficult to build without `Azul Lâmina` because of the `Solar` requirement, but the boss fish itself is needed regardless. Catch every `Água-Viva Imperatriz` that appears and hold them — you may need multiple copies before both recipes are built.

**O Chamado Azul** fires in `Caverna Luminosa`. The boss is `Dragão Azul`, a Legendary fish required for two separate goals: the `Caverna Carmesim` unlock (it must carry `Carmesim` mutation before delivery) and the `Azul Lâmina` tribute recipe (4 copies total, 2 needing `Prometido` and 2 needing `Solar`). Do not deliver any `Dragão Azul` without checking your current stock first — you will need more than one over the course of the endgame. `Carmesim` appears naturally on any fish and can be applied via appraise if needed.

**A Chama Carmesim** fires in `Caverna Carmesim`. The boss is `Dragão Carmesim`, a Legendary fish needed for the `Ruina Prometida` crafting recipe. Clear this hunt while you are working `Caverna Carmesim` for bestiary progress. The recipe also needs `Quimera Rubra` and `Espadarte Carmesim` from the cave, so attending the hunt fits naturally into cave sessions.

**O Último Mito** fires in `Caverna Carmesim` with a 30-minute cooldown and a very low trigger rate. The boss is `Dragão Etéreo`, a Mythic-only encounter. It has no crafting use but offers the rarest rewards in the cave branch. Participate when it fires — you are already in the right pool.

**Escuridao Total...** fires in `As profundezas`. The boss is `Dragão do Profundo`, a Mythic fish that is the primary ingredient for `Vara das Profundezas`. The rest of that recipe — `Cobra do Mar` and `Metal Enferrujado` — are common catches in the same pool and accumulate naturally. The hunt boss is the limiting ingredient. Clear it whenever the hunt fires and hold every `Dragão do Profundo` you catch.

**Encontro com o Maior** fires in `Ponto Zero`. The boss is `Baleia Celeste`, a Mythic fish with one of the longest catch sequences in the game and the highest base value of any single fish. It has no crafting use but is the defining encounter of the Ponto Zero experience. Attending the hunt here should be treated as a priority event. Its loot pool offers strong Mythic rates across the rest of the catch window.

**O Guardiao** fires in `Templo de Micelio`. The boss can appear as either `Mossjaw` (Legendary) or `Awakened Mossjaw` (Mythic), with the Mythic variant being significantly rarer. Both forms have high base values. Clear this hunt while finishing the Micelio bestiary. There is no crafting dependency, but the hunt is a natural part of completing the branch.

## Appraise, Mutations, and Upgrades

### Appraise

Use appraise on fish that already matter:

- mission fish at a bottleneck
- rare fish you want to improve for sale or delivery
- mutation targets
- catches tied to crafting or high-value side goals

Do not burn money appraising random inventory just because the system is available. The value of appraise comes from targeting, not volume.

### Mutations

Treat mutations in three categories:

- **Passive value bonuses** you happily keep and sell. Do not overthink these.
- **Targeted unlock requirements** you actively farm. Identify the rod that produces the mutation you need and use it in the pool most likely to generate candidates.
- **Rod-exclusive projects** that need intentional setup. These include `Brilhante`, `Envenenado`, `Coral`, `Criogenico`, `Espiral`, `Prometido`, `Incinerado`, `Nuvem`, `Cristalizado`, `Profundo`, `Sereno`, and `Arcana`. Each one requires the right rod equipped — not just luck.

The game gets easier once you stop expecting every important mutation to happen by accident.

### Upgrades

Use upgrades to make your favorite rods last longer:

- `control` is the best panic button when catches are becoming too tense
- `luck` is the best broad push when a rod is your main progression tool
- `kg_max` matters when heavy fish are slipping through a rod that otherwise feels right

## Cafeteria

This section is intentionally late because `Cafeteria` is not part of the core 25-pool route.

If you want to see it without stumbling around blindly, the spoiler-light version is simple:

- finish `Angra Cristal` to 100%
- claim the bestiary reward
- pay attention to the special fish that reward gives you
- use the code tied to that fish in pool selection

That is the intended route. `Cafeteria` is a hidden bonus pool, not a main progression checkpoint.

### What to expect

`Cafeteria` is short, secret-flavored content. It is best approached as a victory-lap zone after your real file is already in excellent shape. Do not postpone core cleanup because of it.

It also follows hidden-content rules:

- it is excluded from main pool bestiary completion
- its fish are excluded from main bestiary completion
- the tribute rods tied to it are excluded from rod bestiary completion

That means you should treat it as bonus completion, cosmetics, and curiosity rather than something you owe the save file before calling the run complete.

### Suggestions

- Enter with the expectation of a compact hidden area, not a second full endgame.
- Catch everything there at least once if you enjoy seeing all secret content.
- The `Café` fish from this pool is a crafting ingredient for `Retribuição`. If you are chasing the tribute rods, hold at least one.

## Tribute and Cafeteria Rods

These five rods are excluded from rod bestiary completion. They are not power upgrades you need for progression. They are the game's answer to "what is left when the run is over?" — recipes that require specific hunts, rare mutations, and in some cases materials only found after reaching the Cafeteria. All five crafting recipes become available only after the Cafeteria is unlocked.

None of them are easy. Most of them require either a hunt boss fish, a rod-exclusive mutation, or a fish from a very late zone. Treat them as a long project, not a short checklist.

---

### Azul Lâmina

*For GotinhaAzul.*

**What it needs:** 2 `Dragão Azul` with `Prometido`, 2 `Dragão Azul` with `Solar`, and 15000 money.

`Dragão Azul` is the boss fish of `O Chamado Azul`, which fires in `Caverna Luminosa`. You must catch it during the hunt — it does not appear during normal fishing. You need four copies total, which means attending the hunt multiple times.

`Prometido` is rod-exclusive to `Promessa Luminescente`, `Ruina Prometida`, or `Azul Lâmina` itself. At a base rate of 10%, it is manageable with the right rod equipped throughout your cave sessions. If you have been using `Promessa Luminescente` since `Caverna Luminosa` opened, the `Prometido` copies should accumulate naturally.

`Solar` is the harder ask. Its natural rate is under 0.2%. `Azul Lâmina` itself raises that rate to 3%, creating a bootstrapping situation: the rod makes farming its own recipe significantly easier once you already have it. Until then, expect `Solar` to take patience — either across many sessions of general fishing or targeted sessions after you acquire a first copy of the rod.

---

### Serenidade

*For the Fisch devs.*

**What it needs:** 2 `Peixe-Corda` with `Sereno` from `Profundezas Desoladas`, 1 `Disco de Diamante` with `Sereno` from `Aguas Calmas`, and 1 `Leviata Congelado` with `Congelado` from `Zona Glacial`.

The `Sereno` mutation is the bottleneck. It only appears with `Hollow Dusk` or `Serenidade` equipped. `Hollow Dusk` becomes available after the Cafeteria is reached. Once you have access, equip it in `Profundezas Desoladas` for `Peixe-Corda` targets and in `Aguas Calmas` for `Disco de Diamante`. At 3% per catch, expect to fish each pool for an extended session. `Congelado` appears naturally in cold-zone pools and is not rod-exclusive — any `Leviata Congelado` you caught naturally while finishing `Zona Glacial` may already carry it.

---

### Retribuição

*As Quatro Formas — the four forms.*

**What it needs:** 1 `Gladio Escaldante` with `Carmesim` from `Rachadura Vulcanica`, 1 `Peixe Três-Olhos` with `Espiral` from `As profundezas`, 1 `Lagosta Cristal` with `Cristalizado` from `Angra Cristal`, 1 `Enguia Ância` with `Profundo` from `As profundezas`, and 1 `Café` from `Cafeteria`.

This is the most geographically demanding recipe in the game. Each component comes from a different late zone, and each carries a different mutation requirement. No single piece is unreasonably rare on its own — but gathering all four simultaneously while also collecting a `Café` from the hidden pool means this rod naturally takes the longest to finish.

For the components: `Carmesim` appears naturally on any fish at a low rate, so fishing `Rachadura Vulcanica` over many sessions should eventually produce a `Gladio Escaldante` with `Carmesim`. Use `Vertigem` in `As profundezas` for `Peixe Três-Olhos (Espiral)`. Use `Vara Cristal` in `Angra Cristal` for `Lagosta Cristal (Cristalizado)`. Use `Vara das Profundezas` in `As profundezas` for `Enguia Ância (Profundo)`. The `Café` from `Cafeteria` is required — hold at least one when you visit.

### Sniper

*A joke rod. The description acknowledges this.*

**What it needs:** 1 `Água-Viva Imperatriz`, 20 `Pedra` with `Brilhante`, and 10000 money.

`Água-Viva Imperatriz` is the boss fish of `Eletricidade Desolada` in `Profundezas Desoladas`. You must catch it during the hunt.

`Pedra` is a Common fish in `Grande Recife`. The mutation grind is the real effort — equip `Vara Brilhante` in `Grande Recife` and fish until you have 20 `Pedra` carrying `Brilhante`. At 12.5% chance per catch, expect a long session in the reef. Starting this count early — whenever you are in `Grande Recife` with `Vara Brilhante` for other reasons — means it fills in passively rather than requiring a dedicated farm at the end.

### Mãos

*The simplest tribute recipe. The description is what it is.*

**What it needs:** 3 `Peixe-Mão` from `Pantano Mushgrove`.

If you finished the swamp for `Densa Mata`, you likely already have these. `Peixe-Mão` is not a rare fish. This is the easiest postgame craft by a significant margin.

## Hidden Content Policy

This guide deliberately treats hidden pools and tribute content as bonus completion.

- They exist.
- They are worth seeing.
- They open more naturally for a thorough player than for a rusher.
- They are not part of the cleanest route through the 25 main pools.

If your goal is the most satisfying version of the run, finish the core world first. Then go after the last secrets with a file that already feels complete.

## Final Summary

The best full-completion route in Fisching Overhaul is not the one that rushes the newest unlock. It is the one that keeps the whole file balanced. Push the next branch, keep bestiary progress alive, respect mutation and rod gates, and close pools when they are already nearly done. When you hit a wall, check the Key Bottlenecks block for that stage. The answer is usually a specific fish in a pool you have already visited, or a mutation from a rod you already own.

Every stage in this guide is a chapter you can open mid-run and use immediately. The reference sections at the end back up the stage-level advice with complete lookups on money, crafting, mutations, and hunts. If something in a stage points you to a reference section, that is where the full detail lives.

If you do that, the run carries you naturally from the opening lagoon to the tribute rods at the end — without collapsing into pure cleanup.
